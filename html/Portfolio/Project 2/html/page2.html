3. Osaka

Osaka (pronounced 'Ouh-Sah-ka'), formerly named Naniwa, is one of Japan's most popular prefectures. It is home to a wonderful city, named after the prefecture, called Osaka. So if you're someone who enjoys taking strolls through the metropolitan area, Osaka may be a good stop for you.

Osaka is home to all sorts of different types of food as well; if you're a foodie, this place is one of the most recognized places in Japan when it comes to your favorite pass-time!

3. Osaka

Osaka (pronounced 'Ouh-Sah-Kah'), formerly named Naniwa, is one of Japan's most popular prefectures. It is home to a wonderful city, named after the prefecture, called Osaka. So if you're someone who enjoys taking strolls through the metropolitan area, Osaka may be a good stop for you.

Osaka is home to all sorts of different types of food as well. If you're a foodie, this place is one of the most recognized places in Japan when it comes to your favorite pass-time!

Below are a few pictures of different kinds of food or restaurants you can find throughout Japan, but each prefecture is slightly different on how they prepare and present the dish.

Two other places that are neat to go to while visiting Osaka would be Shinsaibashi-Suji Street and America-Mura.

ShinsaiBashi is a shopping district located south of Osaka Station and sees thousands of shoppers each day!

America Mura, literally meaning 'America Town' is a portion of town that sells things that can be found in America. It's Osaka's little ode to America. These are two pictures that I took. One of which is of one of the most iconic areas in America Mura.
People will come to this place and sit and eat, skateboard, or just come to hang out.
